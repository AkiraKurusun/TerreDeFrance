A Physical Geography Fact

Mont Blanc, on the France–Italy border in the Alps, rises to about 4,808 meters (15,774 feet), making it the highest peak in the Alps and in Western Europe. It sits at the edge of two tectonic plates whose slow collision millions of years ago pushed the Alps upward — the same mountain-building process this site's Plate Tectonics page will explore in more depth.
